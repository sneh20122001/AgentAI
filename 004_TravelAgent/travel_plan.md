# Here's your Travel Plan to Rome üáÆüáπ:

**Day 1: Arrival & Ancient Rome**

*   **Morning (March 1st):**
    *   Arrive at Fiumicino Airport (FCO).
    *   Transfer to your hotel (consider hotels near the city center for easy access to attractions).
    *   Check in and leave your luggage.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Colosseum & Roman Forum:** Explore the iconic Colosseum and wander through the ruins of the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line. (3-4 hours)
    *   **Location:** Piazza del Colosseo, 1, 00184 Roma RM, Italy
    *   **Tickets:** Highly recommended to purchase online beforehand.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner in Monti:** Enjoy a traditional Roman dinner in the Monti neighborhood, known for its trattorias and charming atmosphere. *Armando al Pantheon* is a classic choice. (2-3 hours)
    *   **Restaurant:** Armando al Pantheon (Salita de' Crescenzi, 31, 00186 Roma RM, Italy)

**Day 2: Vatican City & Trastevere Charm**

*   **Morning (March 2nd):**
    *   **Vatican City:** Visit St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums, including the Sistine Chapel. Book your Vatican tickets online well in advance! Consider a guided tour for a more enriching experience. (4-5 hours)
    *   **Location:** Vatican City
    *   **Tickets:** Essential to book online in advance.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Castel Sant'Angelo:** Explore this fascinating cylindrical fortress with stunning views of the city. (2-3 hours)
    *   **Location:** Lungotevere Castello, 50, 00193 Roma RM, Italy
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Trastevere Dinner:** Explore the Trastevere neighborhood and enjoy dinner. This area is known for its lively atmosphere and many restaurants. Try *Roma Sparita* for cacio e pepe (reservations recommended!). (2-3 hours)
    *   **Restaurant:** Roma Sparita (Piazza di Santa Cecilia, 24, 00153 Roma RM, Italy)

**Day 3: Trevi Fountain & Pantheon**

*   **Morning (March 3rd):**
    *   **Trevi Fountain:** Toss a coin into the Trevi Fountain and admire its Baroque beauty. (30-60 minutes)
    *   **Location:** Piazza di Trevi, 00187 Roma RM, Italy
    *   **Pantheon:** Visit the Pantheon, a remarkably preserved ancient Roman temple. (1-2 hours)
    *   **Location:** Piazza della Rotonda, 00186 Roma RM, Italy
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Spanish Steps:** Climb the Spanish Steps and enjoy the views from the top. (1-2 hours)
    *   **Location:** Piazza di Spagna, 00187 Roma RM, Italy
    *   **Shopping on Via Condotti:** Indulge in some window shopping (or real shopping!) on Via Condotti, known for its designer boutiques. (1-2 hours)
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner near the Pantheon:** Find a restaurant near the Pantheon for a delicious meal. Try *Da Armando al Pantheon* if you didn't get a chance on day 1 or explore other options in the area. (2-3 hours)

**Day 4: Borghese Gallery & Gardens**

*   **Morning (March 4th):**
    *   **Borghese Gallery & Museum:** Visit the Borghese Gallery, home to masterpieces by Bernini and Caravaggio. Reservations are *essential* and must be made well in advance. (2 hours - timed entry)
    *   **Location:** Piazzale Scipione Borghese, 5, 00197 Roma RM, Italy
    *   **Tickets:** MANDATORY to book in advance. Timed entry.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Borghese Gardens:** Stroll through the beautiful Borghese Gardens, rent a boat on the lake, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery. (2-3 hours)
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Pizza Night:** Enjoy a casual pizza dinner. *Pizzarium Bonci* is a popular spot for gourmet pizza by the slice. (1-2 hours)
    *   **Restaurant:** Pizzarium Bonci (Via della Meloria, 43, 00136 Roma RM, Italy)

**Day 5: Appian Way & Catacombs**

*   **Morning (March 5th):**
    *   **Appian Way:** Explore the ancient Appian Way by bike or on foot. Rent a bike near the entrance. (3-4 hours)
    *   **Location:** Via Appia Antica
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Catacombs:** Visit the Catacombs of Callixtus or Domitilla, early Christian burial sites. (2-3 hours)
    *   **Location:** Various locations along the Appian Way.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Testaccio Dinner:** Head to the Testaccio neighborhood, known for its authentic Roman cuisine. Try *Flavio al Velavevodetto* for traditional dishes. (2-3 hours)
    *   **Restaurant:** Flavio al Velavevodetto (Via di Monte Testaccio, 97, 00153 Roma RM, Italy)

    *   **Location:** Piazza del Campidoglio, 1, 00186 Roma RM, Italy
    *   **Jewish Ghetto:** Explore the historic Jewish Ghetto and enjoy a traditional Roman-Jewish meal. *Nonna Betta* is a popular choice. (2-3 hours)
     *   **Restaurant:** Nonna Betta (Via del Portico d'Ottavia, 16, 00186 Roma RM, Italy)
